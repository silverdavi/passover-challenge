Here's the Deal

On Seder night, each of you will be asked 20 questions — your own questions, made just for you, at your level. The questions start easy and get harder.

The $100? That's yours if you put in the work. Study a few times a week. Know the stories. Know the Seder. Know why we do what we do. We expect every one of you to get this.

The $300? That's for the kid who goes further. You'll need to study more — about an hour at a time, several times a week. You'll need to understand things deeply, not just remember them.

The $3,000?

Nobody is expected to get this. This prize is almost impossible. To reach 180 points, you'd need to answer nearly every question right — including the hardest ones about obscure midrashim and specific commentators.

Getting there means changing your life for 5 weeks.

Aim for the $100. Dream about the $300.
And if you're crazy enough to go for the $3,000… we'll be watching.

Prize Tiers

Every question is worth its number. Q1 = 1pt, Q2 = 2pts … Q20 = 20pts.
Maximum possible: 210 points.

Bronze $50
Points needed 25+
Study effort ~2 hours total

The warm-up. You showed up, you paid a little attention. But honestly? If you only get Bronze, you didn't really try.

THIS IS THE TARGET
Silver $100
Points needed 50+
Study effort ~5 hours total

Every child is expected to reach this tier. Study 2–3 times a week, about 30 minutes each session, for 3–4 weeks. Know the stories, the Seder, and be able to answer questions about what Pesach means.

Gold $300
Points needed 100+
Study effort ~16 hours total

A real stretch, but within reach. Go beyond the stories and understand the deeper layers — halachot, reasons behind the mitzvot, connections between ideas. It takes discipline, but the payoff is real.

Grand $3,000
Points needed 180+
Study effort ~40–60 hours total

THE MOONSHOT. Nobody is expected to win this. It requires a lifestyle change: 4 sessions/week, 2 hours each, for 5 weeks. Total mastery — every subject, inside and out. If you pull it off, you'll know more about Pesach than most adults.

What to Study

Tap a name to see their study subjects, organized by tier.

Bronze — Basic Familiarity

  1. The Passover Story (basics) — We were slaves in Egypt, Hashem took us out

Silver — Solid Recall

  1. Seder Plate Items — Name each item, what it is, and why it's there

  1. The Four Children — Who they are, what each asks, the answer to each

The Rules

  1. Each child is asked only their own set of 20 questions — no one else's.
  2. Questions are asked in order from easiest to hardest.
  3. No outside help — no books, phones, or whispering.
  4. Partial credit may be given at the questioner's discretion.
  5. You may pass on a question but cannot return to it.
  6. All questions will be based on the study subjects listed above — no surprises.
  7. Prize tiers are cumulative — you get the prize for the highest tier you reach.
