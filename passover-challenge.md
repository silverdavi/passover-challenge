# Passover Learning Challenge 2026

## Overview

A family learning challenge to prepare for Passover. Each child receives a study guide with subjects to learn before Passover eve. On **Passover eve (Seder night)**, they will be quizzed with 20 questions each, tailored to their age and level.

Each child gets **20 questions** in increasing difficulty:

## Study Subjects by Child

> **Key**: Subjects are listed from basic → advanced. For the **Grand Prize (180 pts)**, a child must know *all* their subjects deeply — not just recognize the topic, but be able to explain details, give examples, and answer tricky "why" and "what if" questions about them.

### Emily (Age 4) — Beginner

**Bronze level (basic familiarity):**
1. **The Passover Story (basics)** — We were slaves in Egypt, Hashem took us out
2. **The Four Questions (Ma Nishtana)** — Memorize and understand the meaning of each question
3. **The 10 Plagues** — Names and basic idea of each, in order

**Silver level (solid recall):**
4. **Seder Plate Items** — Name each item, what it is, and why it's there (maror, charoset, shank bone, egg, karpas, matzah)
5. **Matzah vs. Chametz** — Why we eat matzah, what chametz is, examples of each
6. **Moshe Rabbeinu** — Baby in the basket, who found him, the burning bush, leading the people out

**Gold level (understanding and explanation):**
7. **The Four Children** — Who are the four sons, what does each one ask, what's the answer to each
8. **Dayenu** — Sing the song, know what at least 5 of the stanzas mean
9. **Eliyahu HaNavi** — Who is Eliyahu, why do we open the door, what is the cup for

**Grand level (deep for her age):**
10. **Afikoman** — What it represents, why we hide it, what part of the matzah it comes from
11. **The Seder night sequence** — What comes first, second, third (Kadesh, Urchatz, Karpas, Yachatz, Maggid...)
12. **Retell the full story** — From Yaakov going to Egypt through leaving Egypt, in her own words, with details

### Daniel (Age 7) — Beginner-Intermediate

All of Emily's subjects (at deeper understanding), plus:

**Bronze level:**
1. **The Passover Story (detailed)** — Yosef in Egypt, how the slavery started, Pharaoh's decrees, the midwives Shifra and Puah
2. **The 10 Plagues (details)** — Exact order, what happened during each, which ones the magicians could copy, warnings to Pharaoh

**Silver level:**
3. **The Seder Order (15 steps)** — Name all 15 steps in order, know what happens at each one
4. **Crossing the Red Sea** — What happened, who was Nachshon, the Song of the Sea (Az Yashir)
5. **The Three Matzot** — Why three, what they represent (Kohen, Levi, Yisrael)

**Gold level:**
6. **Maror and Charoset** — What do they symbolize, why together in Korech, what's in charoset and why
7. **The Haggadah Structure** — What is Maggid, the story of the five Rabbis in Bnei Brak, Avadim Hayinu
8. **Korban Pesach** — What animal, when was it brought, who could eat it, what rules applied

**Grand level:**
9. **The Jews in the Desert** — Manna (details: how it fell, the double portion, what it tasted like), water from the rock, clouds of glory, the 40 years
10. **Freedom and Gratitude** — Why we celebrate, what freedom means in Judaism, why we must see ourselves as if *we* left Egypt
11. **Moshe's life in detail** — Midyan, the daughters of Yitro, coming back to Egypt, Aharon as spokesman, Moshe's humility
12. **The night of the Exodus** — What exactly happened: the korban, the blood on the doorposts, eating in haste, the firstborn

### Eliyahu (Age 11) — Intermediate-Advanced

All of Daniel's subjects (at deeper understanding), plus:

**Bronze level:**
1. **Halachot of Pesach (basics)** — Bedikat chametz (when, how, the candle and feather), biur chametz, mechira (selling chametz)
2. **The Four Cups of Wine** — What they represent (the four leshonot of geulah), which brachos, when we drink them

**Silver level:**
3. **The Haggadah in Depth** — Arami Oved Avi passage: what it means, how the Rabbis darshen each phrase
4. **Pesach in the Torah** — Parshat Bo, Re'eh, Emor — which mitzvot come from where
5. **Hallel** — Why we say it at the Seder, why it's split (half before the meal, half after), why we don't say full Hallel on the last days

**Gold level:**
6. **Counting the Omer** — When it starts, what it commemorates, connection to Matan Torah, the mourning period and why
7. **Yetziat Mitzrayim in Daily Life** — Where the Exodus is mentioned: Shema, Kiddush Friday night, the third paragraph of Shema, brachot
8. **Stories of the Sages** — The Seder in Bnei Brak (who were the five Rabbis, what were they discussing, why did the students interrupt), Rabbi Elazar ben Azarya's age

**Grand level:**
9. **Miriam and the Women** — Miriam watching the basket, her prophecy, her well in the desert, the women's tambourines (where did they get them?), why the women's faith was greater
10. **Pesach Sheini** — What it is, who brought it, the story of the impure people who came to Moshe, its spiritual message ("it's never too late")
11. **Makkat Bechorot details** — Midnight, who counts as a bechor, why Pharaoh himself survived, what happened to the Egyptian gods
12. **The structure of the Haggadah** — Why we start with shame (Avadim Hayinu / Mitchila ovdei avodah zarah) and end with praise, the debate between Rav and Shmuel
13. **The Exodus timeline** — From Rosh Hashana (when the slavery ended) through Nissan, the specific dates of events

### Yerachmiel (Age 12) — Advanced

All of Eliyahu's subjects (at deeper understanding), plus:

**Bronze level:**
1. **Halachot of Pesach (advanced)** — Kitniyot (who holds what, why), gebrokts (what it is, who is machmir), shi'urim (kezayit for matzah, maror; revi'it for wine — how much is each?)
2. **The Rambam on Pesach** — Key halachot from Hilchot Chametz U'Matzah: the 7 chapters, what topics they cover, a few specific halachot inside

**Silver level:**
3. **Philosophical Themes** — Free will vs. hardening of Pharaoh's heart (the Rambam's approach, the Ramban's approach), why were 10 plagues necessary, midah k'neged midah in the plagues
4. **Midrashim on the Exodus** — The wealth they took from Egypt (and the promise to Avraham), the Erev Rav (who were they, what problems did they cause), the 50/200/250 plagues at the sea (R' Yosi / R' Akiva / R' Eliezer)
5. **Pesach and Geulah** — The four geulot, connection between the first redemption and the final one, why is the Seder night called "leil shimurim"

**Gold level:**
6. **The Arizal's Seder customs** — Why we don't dip the matzah, the kavanos behind the steps, the concept of mochin (spiritual consciousness) at each stage
7. **Commentators on the Haggadah** — Specific insights from: the Vilna Gaon (the connection of the 15 steps to Shir HaMaalot), the Maharal (Gevurot Hashem — the structure of the plagues in 3 groups), the Abarbanel (his 100 questions)
8. **Historical Pesach Observance** — How the Korban Pesach worked in Temple times (the three groups, the Levi'im singing Hallel, the roasting), Pesach in exile through history (Marranos, the Holocaust, early Israel)

**Grand level:**
9. **Shir HaShirim** — Why it's read on Pesach, who authored it, its deeper meaning (the love between Hashem and Israel), R' Akiva's statement that it's the "Holy of Holies" of all Tanach
10. **Chametz as a Spiritual Concept** — The Zohar on chametz and matzah, the yetzer hara as "se'or she'ba'isa," why just a mashehu (tiny amount) of chametz is forbidden but matzah has a shi'ur
11. **Textual analysis** — The differences between the Pesach story as told in Shemot vs. Devarim vs. the Haggadah — what's included, what's left out, and why (why is Moshe barely mentioned in the Haggadah?)
12. **The halachic sugyot** — Pesachim: matza ashira, matzah of mitzvah, the debate about when the obligation to eat matzah begins, the difference between the first night and the rest of Pesach
13. **Unusual / deep questions** — Why specifically Egypt? The spiritual significance of Mitzrayim (meitzarim = boundaries). Why were the Jews in Egypt for 210 years and not 400. The bris bein habetarim and its prophecy.

## Question Difficulty Breakdown

Each child's 20 questions follow this structure. The difficulty ramps **steeply** — the top questions are designed so that only a child who has truly put in serious hours will know the answers.

| Questions | Difficulty | Points | Cumulative | Study Needed | Question Style |
|-----------|-----------|--------|------------|-------------|----------------|
| 1–3 | Trivial | 1–3 | 6 | Almost none | "What is...?" — single-fact recall, yes/no |
| 4–7 | Easy | 4–7 | 28 | ~2 hrs total | Name a list, basic "who/what/where", retell a story |
| 8–10 | Medium | 8–10 | 55 | ~5 hrs total | Explain a concept, connect two ideas, "why do we...?" |
| 11–14 | Hard | 11–14 | 105 | ~15 hrs total | Multi-part answers, deeper "why", compare/contrast, know specific details |
| 15–17 | Very Hard | 15–17 | 153 | ~30 hrs total | Precise sources, lesser-known midrashim/halachot, explain a machloket or reason behind a minhag |
| 18–20 | Expert | 18–20 | 210 | ~60 hrs total | Obscure details, textual analysis, synthesis across multiple sources, "what does X commentator say about Y" |

**Total: 210 points**

### Design philosophy

- **Bronze (25 pts)**: The "you showed up" tier. A child who casually pays attention can get this. If you *only* get Bronze, it means you barely tried.
- **Silver (50 pts)**: **The real target.** Every child should reach this with reasonable effort — a few study sessions a week. This is the bar we're setting: learn the material, know it, be ready. $100 is the reward for doing what's expected.
- **Gold (100 pts)**: A genuine achievement. Not expected, but absolutely within reach for a motivated child. This takes *commitment* — studying regularly, going deeper, understanding the *why* behind things. If your child gets Gold, be proud. They earned it.
- **Grand (180 pts)**: **The moonshot.** This is not a normal prize tier. This is: "What if a child decided to spend 5 weeks living and breathing Pesach Torah?" 4 study sessions a week. 2 hours each. Mastering texts, commentaries, halachot, midrashim. The questions at the top are designed to be *brutally hard* — even for someone who has studied. If a child reaches 180, they will have accomplished something extraordinary. The $3,000 is not a gift — it is recognition that they did something almost no one would do.

## Rules

1. Each child is asked only **their own** set of 20 questions — no one else's.
2. Questions are asked **in order** from easiest to hardest.
3. No outside help — no books, phones, or whispering.
4. Partial credit may be given at the questioner's discretion.
5. The child may **pass** on a question but cannot return to it.
6. All questions will be based on the study subjects listed above — no surprises.
7. Prize tiers are **cumulative** — you get the prize for the highest tier you reach.
